Road trips are funny things.  You never know what's going to happen or where the conversations are going to go.  Thankfully, between George, G and I, we were able to keep it going around one of my favorite subjects.  No wait...two of them.  And that's awesome.

After a great day at Jazz Fest where I totally [grooved ](http://www.flickr.com/photos/snoopykiss/10810235/in/photostream/) on the Gospel Tent.  There was also a [B-day](http://www.flickr.com/photos/snoopykiss/10809693/) and [other](http://www.flickr.com/photos/snoopykiss/10809656/) celebration party which involved lots of cool people and then a late night run to the French Quarter and closing down 3 bars, one right after the other.  And some [dancing on the pool table.](http://www.flickr.com/photos/snoopykiss/10809924/)

Day 3 involved being all nostalgic about Tulane while coming across some [very](http://www.flickr.com/photos/snoopykiss/10810184/) interesting [sidewalk ](http://www.flickr.com/photos/snoopykiss/10810176/) musings.  That and lunch with the family.  

One of the most amazing parts for me was that for the first time ever, I actually wanted to stay.  Most everyone has heard me complain about NO, but this time things were different.  Or I was.  Whatever.  I enjoyed it immensely.
